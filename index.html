Market Crystallization Point

The period from February 2 to 8 became a crystallization point for several long-term trends: a shortage of sunflower oil supply in the Black Sea region, a regulatory respite in the EU due to the postponement of EUDR, and the rapid rise of Central Asian countries as new export hubs. Conclusion & Forecast

1. Price Stabilization at Highs: Sunflower oil remains a deficit asset through H1 2026. Market will balance via substitution with cheaper oils.

2. Regulatory Landscape: The EUDR delay provides a respite but digitalization of supply chain passports remains a competitive necessity.

3. Central Asian Growth: Kazakhstan set to become a Top 3 global exporter by 2027 as it moves from raw seeds to finished oil exports.

4. Energy Link: Correlation with oil prices remains tight; US-Iran de-escalation will be a key driver for Soy and Rape oil prices.
