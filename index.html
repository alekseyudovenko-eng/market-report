Weekly Overview: Jan 26 - Feb 2, 2026

The vegetable oils market witnessed mixed volatility this week. While Sunflower Oil (FOB Black Sea) maintained its premium due to constrained crushing volumes in Ukraine, Palm Oil futures retreated slightly on improved production forecasts from Malaysia. In Europe, logistical bottlenecks persist at the German-Polish border, affecting rapeseed oil transit. Policy discussions are dominated by the delayed implementation nuances of the EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR), creating uncertainty for importers in Rotterdam and Hamburg.

Infrastructure Alerts & Risk Assessment

Policy & Regulatory Changes

Analysis of regulatory shifts in EU and Central Asia

Extended Analysis: Impact of Regulation

The regulatory landscape in early 2026 is defined by two major axes: the implementation hurdles of the European Green Deal components (specifically EUDR) and the protectionist measures emerging in Central Asia to secure domestic food security.

Context: As of Jan 2026, full compliance is mandatory for large operators.

Current Event: Importers in the Netherlands are reporting delays in IT system integration for "Due Diligence Statements". Several shipments of Palm Oil derivatives from Indonesia are currently held in bonded warehouses awaiting geo-location verification clearance.

Impact: High for Importers

Context: Uzbekistan aims to support local sunflower crushing.

Current Event: The Ministry of Economy proposed reinstating a 12% VAT on imported bottled sunflower oil, previously exempted. Bulk crude oil imports for local refining retain zero-rated status. This shift favors importing raw material over finished goods.

Impact: Shift to Crude Imports

Context: Monthly review of export limits.

Current Event: The Ministry of Agriculture confirmed the export quota for Sunflower Seed will remain at 20% of historical production for February 2026. No restrictions on Sunflower Oil exports, encouraging value-added processing.

Impact: Stable

Focus: Biofuel Mandates

Germany is debating a reduction in the cap for crop-based biofuels for Q2 2026. If passed, this could reduce demand for Rapeseed Oil by an estimated 50,000 MT/month.

Probability of enactment: 65%
